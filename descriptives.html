Much more effective is to use the loess smoother to show the relationship between continuous variables and FEV1. Marginal distributions of age and height are shown using extended box plots at the top of the plots. Feint tick marks show the data density of age and height for each smoking group.

par(mfrow=c(1,2))   # one row, two column graphic
summaryRc(fev ~ age + height + stratify(smoke), data=FEV,
          label.curves=list(keys='lines', cex=.5), bpplot='top', nloc=list(x=.9, y=.1))
